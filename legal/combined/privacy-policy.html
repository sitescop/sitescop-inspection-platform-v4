Privacy Policy

SiteScop Pty Ltd is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This Privacy Policy explains how personal information is collected, used, stored and disclosed when providing Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection services.

1. Information We Collect

Depending on the services requested, we may collect:

  • Client name.
  • Postal address.
  • Inspection property address.
  • Email address.
  • Telephone and mobile numbers.
  • Inspection booking details.
  • Building Inspection Reports.
  • Timber Pest Inspection Reports.
  • Photographs and thermal images.
  • Electronic signatures.
  • Communication records.
  • Payment information where applicable.

2. How Information is Collected

Personal information may be collected through:

  • Inspection booking forms.
  • Our website.
  • Email correspondence.
  • Telephone conversations.
  • SMS communications.
  • Client Inspection Agreement forms.
  • Inspection Reports.
  • Face-to-face meetings.
Only information reasonably necessary to provide our services is collected and retained.

3. How We Use Your Information

SiteScop Pty Ltd collects and uses personal information only for purposes reasonably necessary to provide our Building and Timber Pest Inspection services. Your information may be used to:

  • Arrange inspection appointments.
  • Conduct Building Inspections.
  • Conduct Timber Pest Inspections.
  • Prepare and deliver Inspection Reports.
  • Communicate regarding bookings and inspections.
  • Issue invoices and receive payments.
  • Respond to enquiries and customer support requests.
  • Meet legal, regulatory and insurance obligations.
  • Improve our services, systems and customer experience.

4. Disclosure of Personal Information

SiteScop Pty Ltd does not sell, rent or trade personal information to third parties. Information may be disclosed where reasonably necessary to:

  • Professional advisers.
  • Cloud storage providers.
  • Email and communication providers.
  • Payment service providers.
  • Government authorities where required by law.
  • Courts, tribunals or regulatory bodies where legally required.
Personal information will only be disclosed where authorised or required under Australian law.

5. Storage and Security

SiteScop Pty Ltd takes reasonable administrative, technical and physical measures to protect personal information from loss, misuse, unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration or destruction. Personal information may be stored electronically using secure computer systems and reputable cloud-based services. Access is restricted to authorised personnel who require the information to perform their duties.

6. Retention of Information

Inspection records, reports, photographs and related documentation may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to satisfy business, legal, insurance and regulatory requirements. When information is no longer required, reasonable steps will be taken to securely destroy or permanently de-identify the information.

7. Access and Correction

Clients may request access to the personal information held by SiteScop Pty Ltd and may request correction of inaccurate, incomplete or outdated information, subject to applicable Australian law.

8. Website and Cookies

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to improve website functionality, analyse website traffic and enhance the user experience. Users may disable cookies through their browser settings, although some website features may not function correctly.

9. Changes to this Privacy Policy

SiteScop Pty Ltd may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in legislation, business practices or our services. The most recent version published forms the current Privacy Policy.
