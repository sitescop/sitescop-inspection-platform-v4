Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection Limitations

This document explains the limitations that apply to the Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection and Inspection Report. The inspection is a non-invasive visual inspection only and is limited by accessibility, visibility and the condition of the property at the time of inspection.

1. Visual Inspection Only

The inspection is limited to the visible and readily accessible areas of the property. No destructive testing, dismantling or removal of building components will be carried out. The Inspector will not remove wall linings, ceiling linings, floor coverings, insulation, furniture, stored goods or other obstructions to inspect concealed areas.

Hidden structural defects, concealed moisture problems and concealed timber pest activity may remain undetected.

2. Readily Accessible Areas

Only areas that can be safely accessed without risk of injury or damage to the property will be inspected. Inspection may be restricted by:

  • Locked rooms.
  • Stored goods.
  • Furniture.
  • Heavy appliances.
  • Wall linings.
  • Ceiling linings.
  • Floor coverings.
  • Insulation.
  • Restricted roof access.
  • Restricted subfloor clearance.
  • Unsafe structural conditions.
  • Animal activity.
Areas that are inaccessible cannot be inspected and therefore cannot be reported upon.

3. Roof Spaces

Roof spaces are inspected only where safe and practical access is available. The Inspector will not enter roof spaces where access is unsafe or where there is a risk of injury or damage to the property. Inspection may be restricted by:

  • Limited access openings.
  • Low roof clearance.
  • Excessive insulation.
  • Unsafe roof framing.
  • Electrical hazards.
  • Extreme temperatures.
  • Fragile ceiling linings.

4. Subfloor Areas

Subfloor inspections are limited to areas that are safely accessible. Inspection may be restricted by:

  • Insufficient clearance.
  • Standing water.
  • Stored materials.
  • Unsafe structural conditions.
  • Electrical hazards.
  • Animal activity.
  • Excessive debris.
Where access to roof spaces or subfloor areas is restricted, building defects, termite activity, timber damage or moisture problems may remain undetected.

5. Concealed Defects

The inspection cannot identify defects concealed by building materials, finishes, insulation, furniture, stored goods, vegetation or any other obstruction. Examples include:

  • Hidden structural movement.
  • Concealed framing defects.
  • Hidden plumbing leaks.
  • Concealed moisture ingress.
  • Defects within wall cavities.
  • Defects beneath floor coverings.
  • Concealed roof leaks.

6. Concealed Timber Pest Activity

Timber pests frequently remain concealed within wall cavities, roof framing, floor framing and other enclosed building elements. The inspection cannot determine whether concealed termite activity or timber damage exists where direct visual evidence is unavailable. No visual inspection can locate every infestation.

7. Weather Conditions

Weather conditions before or during the inspection may affect the ability to identify building defects, moisture ingress and timber pest activity. Heavy rain, recent rainfall, poor lighting, excessive moisture or extreme temperatures may reduce the effectiveness of the inspection. Some defects or timber pest activity may only become apparent under different environmental conditions.

8. Occupied Properties

Where the property is occupied, furniture, stored goods, personal belongings, appliances and floor coverings may prevent complete inspection of all building components. The Inspector is not required to move furniture, appliances, stored items or personal possessions. Areas concealed by such items are excluded from the inspection.

The Client should ensure the property is as accessible as possible before the inspection to maximise the effectiveness of the inspection.

9. Termite Management Systems

Where termite management systems are visible, the Inspector may comment on their apparent presence and general condition. The inspection does not certify the design, installation, effectiveness, compliance or remaining service life of any termite management system.

Examples include:

  • Chemical management systems.
  • Physical barriers.
  • Stainless steel mesh systems.
  • Graded stone systems.
  • Inspection zones.
  • Reticulation systems.
  • Termite baiting systems.
The presence of a termite management system does not guarantee that termites will not attack the property. Regular inspections remain essential.

10. Timber Damage Assessment

The inspection identifies only visible timber damage. The Inspector does not determine the structural adequacy, engineering performance or remaining strength of damaged timber. Where significant timber damage is identified, further assessment by a Structural Engineer, Licensed Builder or another appropriately qualified professional may be recommended.

11. Moisture Observations

Moisture readings obtained during the inspection are intended only as an aid to identifying areas that may require further investigation. Moisture meters and thermal imaging cameras cannot determine:

  • The exact source of moisture.
  • The duration of moisture exposure.
  • Hidden plumbing leaks.
  • Future moisture ingress.
  • The extent of concealed damage.
  • Whether timber decay has commenced.

12. Environmental Conditions

The inspection does not include assessment of environmental conditions including:

  • Asbestos-containing materials.
  • Lead-based paint.
  • Mould identification or laboratory testing.
  • Indoor air quality.
  • Hazardous substances.
  • Soil contamination.
  • Geotechnical conditions.
  • Underground services.
Separate specialist inspections should be obtained where these matters are important to the Client.

13. Future Changes

The Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection Report represents the visible condition of the property only at the date and time of inspection. Building defects, moisture problems and timber pest activity may develop or become apparent after the inspection due to weather conditions, maintenance, renovations, ground movement, normal deterioration or other factors beyond the Inspector's control. No inspection can predict future building performance or future timber pest activity.

14. Recommendations

Where significant building defects, active timber pests, timber damage, excessive moisture, structural movement or conditions conducive to timber pest attack are identified, the Inspector may recommend further investigation or remedial work by appropriately qualified specialists. Recommendations may include:

  • Structural Engineer.
  • Licensed Builder.
  • Licensed Pest Management Technician.
  • Licensed Electrician.
  • Licensed Plumber.
  • Roofing Contractor.
  • Waterproofing Specialist.
  • Drainage Specialist.
  • Geotechnical Engineer.
  • Other suitably qualified consultants.

15. Client Acknowledgement

By proceeding with this Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection, the Client acknowledges and accepts that:

  • The inspection is visual and non-invasive.
  • Only readily accessible areas can be inspected.
  • Hidden structural defects may exist.
  • Hidden timber pest activity may exist.
  • Concealed moisture problems may remain undetected.
  • The inspection reflects the condition of the property only at the date and time of inspection.
  • Recommendations contained within the Inspection Report should be acted upon promptly.
  • Regular building maintenance and routine timber pest inspections remain essential.
Failure to obtain recommended specialist advice or undertake recommended repairs or timber pest treatment may result in additional property damage, increased repair costs or ongoing timber pest activity.

16. Applicable Standards

This Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection has been undertaken generally in accordance with the principles of:

  • Australian Standard AS 4349.1 – Inspection of Buildings – Pre-Purchase Inspections – Residential Buildings.
  • Australian Standard AS 4349.3 – Inspection of Buildings – Timber Pest Inspections.

Where any variation from these Standards has been agreed with the Client, that variation will be clearly stated within the Inspection Agreement or Inspection Report.
