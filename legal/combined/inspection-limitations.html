Inspection Limitations

This inspection is a visual, non-invasive inspection only. It is limited to those areas of the property that are reasonably accessible and visible at the time of the inspection.

No part of the property will be dismantled, removed, damaged or disturbed during the inspection unless specifically agreed in writing before the inspection is undertaken.

Reasonably Accessible Areas

The inspection is limited to areas that can be safely accessed by the inspector using normal inspection methods and equipment. Areas that cannot be safely accessed or viewed will not be inspected.

Examples include, but are not limited to:

  • Roof spaces with insufficient access or clearance.
  • Subfloor areas with inadequate clearance.
  • Locked rooms or locked structures.
  • Areas obstructed by furniture or stored goods.
  • Concealed wall cavities.
  • Ceiling cavities where access is unavailable.
  • Areas hidden behind insulation.
  • Areas covered by floor coverings or finishes.
  • Areas concealed by vegetation.
  • Areas considered unsafe due to structural damage or other hazards.

Building Inspection Limitations

The Building Inspection is not a guarantee that every defect will be identified. Some defects may be hidden, concealed or may not be apparent at the time of inspection.

The inspection does not include:

  • Engineering certification.
  • Building design assessment.
  • Compliance with building approvals.
  • Council approval verification.
  • Surveying services.
  • Property valuation.
  • Cost estimates for repairs.
  • Electrical system testing.
  • Gas installation testing.
  • Plumbing performance testing.
  • Mechanical services testing.
  • Swimming pool compliance inspections unless specifically included.
  • Asbestos identification unless specifically requested.
  • Hazardous materials assessment.

Timber Pest Inspection Limitations

The Timber Pest Inspection is limited to the detection of evidence that is visible at the time of inspection.

Timber pests may be present inside concealed wall cavities, beneath floor coverings, inside structural timbers or other inaccessible locations without visible evidence.

The absence of visible evidence does not guarantee that timber pests are not present.

The inspection does not include:

  • Destructive inspection.
  • Removal of wall linings.
  • Removal of floor coverings.
  • Removal of insulation.
  • Excavation.
  • Removal of stored goods.
  • Cutting or drilling of building elements.

Weather Conditions

Weather conditions may affect the inspection. Heavy rain, poor lighting, excessive moisture or other environmental conditions may restrict access or reduce the visibility of certain defects or timber pest evidence.

Furniture and Stored Goods

Furniture, appliances, personal belongings, storage items and other movable objects are not moved during the inspection.

Defects concealed by these items cannot be reported.

Future Conditions

The inspection report represents the condition of the property only at the date and time of inspection.

Property conditions may change after the inspection due to weather events, movement, renovations, maintenance, occupancy or timber pest activity.

Accordingly, the inspection report should not be relied upon as a guarantee of the future condition of the property.

Further Investigation

Where the inspector identifies significant defects, evidence of timber pests or conditions that cannot be fully assessed during the inspection, SiteScop may recommend further investigation by an appropriately qualified specialist before any purchase or major decision is made.

Client Acknowledgement

The Client acknowledges that no visual inspection can identify every defect or every instance of timber pest activity. The inspection is limited by the accessibility of the property and by the condition of the building at the time of inspection.
