Scope of Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection

This Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection has been requested by the Client and will be carried out by SiteScop Pty Ltd. The inspection combines a visual Building Inspection together with a visual Timber Pest Inspection and is intended to provide the Client with a comprehensive assessment of the property at the date and time of inspection. The inspection is limited to the readily accessible areas of the property and to conditions visible at the time of the inspection.

1. Purpose of Inspection

The purpose of this inspection is to identify visible major building defects, significant structural issues, safety hazards, evidence of timber pest activity, timber damage, conditions conducive to timber pest attack and other visible conditions requiring further investigation. The inspection assists the Client in making an informed decision regarding the condition of the property but does not guarantee that every defect or timber pest issue will be identified.

This inspection combines the principles of Australian Standards AS 4349.1 (Pre-Purchase Building Inspection) and AS 4349.3 (Timber Pest Inspection) where applicable.

2. Scope of Inspection

The inspection is a non-invasive visual assessment of the readily accessible areas of the property. No destructive testing, dismantling, removal of finishes, excavation or invasive investigation will be undertaken unless specifically agreed in writing.

3. Building Components Included

Where safe and readily accessible, the Building Inspection may include visual assessment of:

  • Site and surrounding grounds.
  • Foundations and visible footings.
  • External walls.
  • Internal walls.
  • Floors.
  • Ceilings.
  • Roof exterior.
  • Roof space.
  • Subfloor.
  • Windows.
  • External and internal doors.
  • Garage and carport.
  • Balconies.
  • Verandahs.
  • Pergolas.
  • Retaining walls where applicable.
  • Paths and driveways.
  • Visible structural elements.
  • General moisture observations.

4. Timber Pest Components Included

Where safe and readily accessible, the Timber Pest Inspection may include visual assessment for:

  • Active termites.
  • Evidence of previous termite activity.
  • Termite mud shelter tubes.
  • Visible termite damage.
  • Wood borers.
  • Wood decay fungi.
  • Previous timber pest treatments.
  • Conditions conducive to timber pest attack.
  • Visible termite management systems.
  • Timber-to-soil contact.
  • Excessive moisture.
  • Poor drainage.
  • Cellulose debris.

5. Inspection Areas

Where access is safe and practical, the inspection may include:

  • Interior living areas.
  • Exterior building elements.
  • Roof void.
  • Subfloor.
  • Garage.
  • Carport.
  • Attached decks.
  • Pergolas.
  • Fences attached to the dwelling.
  • Timber retaining walls adjacent to the dwelling.
  • Garden beds adjoining the building.
  • Immediate site surrounds.
Only areas that are safe and readily accessible at the time of inspection can be inspected.

6. Inspection Equipment

The Inspector may use professional non-invasive equipment to assist the inspection including:

  • High-powered LED inspection torch.
  • Moisture meter.
  • Thermal imaging camera (where applicable).
  • Sounding tool.
  • Probe or donger.
  • Inspection mirror.
  • Digital camera.
  • Ladder where safe and practical.

The use of inspection equipment assists the visual inspection but does not guarantee that concealed defects or concealed timber pest activity will be detected.

7. Major Building Defects

The Building Inspection includes the identification of visible major defects that, in the opinion of the Inspector, require significant repair, replacement or further investigation. Major defects may affect the structural integrity, serviceability, safety or durability of the building.

8. Minor Building Defects

Where observed, the Inspector may also report visible minor defects including maintenance items, normal wear and tear, minor deterioration and cosmetic defects that do not significantly affect the overall performance of the building.

9. Structural Observations

The inspection includes visual observations of accessible structural elements for signs of movement, cracking, settlement, deflection, distortion, deterioration or other conditions that may indicate structural concerns. The inspection is not a structural engineering assessment and does not certify the structural adequacy of the building.

10. Timber Pest Findings

The Timber Pest Inspection includes reporting of visible evidence of:

  • Active termite activity.
  • Previous termite activity.
  • Termite shelter tubes.
  • Timber damage.
  • Wood borers.
  • Wood decay fungi.
  • Previous timber pest treatments.
  • Conditions conducive to timber pest attack.
  • Visible deficiencies in termite management systems.

11. Moisture and Water Ingress

Where visible evidence of moisture ingress, plumbing leaks, poor drainage or excessive moisture is observed, these conditions may be reported because they may contribute to building deterioration and increase the risk of timber pest attack. Where appropriate, a non-invasive moisture meter and thermal imaging camera may be used as inspection aids. Moisture readings and thermal images are indicative only and cannot determine the exact source, duration or cause of moisture.

The absence of visible moisture, structural defects or timber pest activity does not guarantee that concealed defects or concealed infestations do not exist.

12. Termite Management Systems

Where visible, the Inspector may comment on the apparent presence and general condition of termite management systems including chemical barriers, physical barriers, inspection zones and baiting systems. The inspection does not certify the effectiveness, serviceability or remaining life of any termite management system.

13. Items Specifically Excluded

Unless specifically agreed in writing, this Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection does not include:

  • Destructive or invasive inspection.
  • Opening wall, ceiling or floor cavities.
  • Removing insulation, floor coverings or wall linings.
  • Moving heavy furniture, appliances or stored goods.
  • Excavation of soil or landscaping.
  • Inspection beneath concrete slabs.
  • Electrical testing.
  • Gas testing.
  • Plumbing testing.
  • Swimming pools and spas.
  • Solar systems.
  • Security systems.
  • Building code compliance.
  • Engineering certification.
  • Valuation of the property.
  • Environmental hazards including asbestos, lead paint and mould testing.
  • Geotechnical investigations.
The inspection is limited to visible and readily accessible areas only. Concealed defects, hidden structural problems and concealed timber pest infestations may exist that cannot reasonably be identified during a visual inspection.

14. Recommendations

Where significant building defects, timber pest activity, conditions conducive to timber pest attack or other matters requiring specialist assessment are identified, the Inspector may recommend further investigation by appropriately qualified professionals. Recommendations may include consultation with:

  • Structural Engineer.
  • Licensed Builder.
  • Licensed Electrician.
  • Licensed Plumber.
  • Licensed Pest Management Technician.
  • Roofing Contractor.
  • Waterproofing Specialist.
  • Drainage Specialist.
  • Other suitably qualified consultants.

15. Client Acknowledgement

By proceeding with this Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection, the Client acknowledges and accepts that:

  • The inspection is visual and non-invasive.
  • Only readily accessible areas can be inspected.
  • Concealed defects and concealed timber pest activity may remain undetected.
  • The inspection represents the condition of the property only at the time of inspection.
  • Recommendations contained within the Inspection Report should be acted upon promptly.
  • Regular building maintenance and annual timber pest inspections remain essential.

16. Applicable Standards

This Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection has been undertaken generally in accordance with the principles of:

  • Australian Standard AS 4349.1 – Inspection of Buildings – Pre-Purchase Inspections – Residential Buildings.
  • Australian Standard AS 4349.3 – Inspection of Buildings – Timber Pest Inspections.

Where any variation from these Standards has been agreed with the Client, that variation will be recorded in the Inspection Agreement or Inspection Report.

End of Combined Building & Timber Pest Inspection Scope
