Scope of Inspection

This Inspection Agreement applies to a Combined Building Inspection and Timber Pest Inspection requested by the Client and performed by SiteScop Pty Ltd ("SiteScop").

The inspection consists of two separate inspections carried out during the same visit:

  • A Building Inspection conducted generally in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.1 – Inspection of Buildings.
  • A Timber Pest Inspection conducted generally in accordance with Australian Standard AS 4349.3 – Inspection of Buildings – Timber Pest Inspections.

Purpose of the Inspection

The purpose of the inspection is to provide the Client with an independent visual assessment of the condition of the property at the time of inspection. The inspection is intended to identify significant observable defects, timber pest activity, timber pest damage, conditions conducive to timber pest attack and other relevant matters that are visible and reasonably accessible.

The inspection assists the Client in making informed decisions regarding the purchase, ownership, maintenance or management of the property. It is not intended to predict future performance of the building or guarantee the absence of defects or timber pests.

Building Inspection

The Building Inspection includes a visual assessment of the property's major structural elements and accessible components, including where reasonably accessible:

  • Site and drainage.
  • Foundations.
  • External walls.
  • Internal walls.
  • Floors.
  • Roof exterior.
  • Roof framing.
  • Ceilings.
  • Doors and windows.
  • Bathrooms, laundry and kitchen.
  • Balconies, decks and external structures where included.

The inspection is designed to identify significant defects, structural movement, safety hazards and major maintenance issues that are visible at the time of inspection.

Timber Pest Inspection

The Timber Pest Inspection includes a visual assessment for evidence of:

  • Subterranean termites.
  • Dampwood termites.
  • Drywood termites where applicable.
  • Wood borers.
  • Fungal decay.
  • Conditions conducive to timber pest attack.
  • Previous timber pest damage.

Where evidence of timber pests or damage is identified, recommendations may be made for further investigation, treatment or monitoring.

Inspection Method

The inspection is a visual and non-destructive inspection only. SiteScop will not dismantle, remove, damage or disturb any part of the building, finishes, fixtures, furniture, stored goods or landscaping unless specifically agreed in writing before the inspection.

Inspection equipment such as moisture meters, thermal imaging cameras, sounding tools, probing tools, ladders and other non-destructive instruments may be used where appropriate.

Areas Included

Where reasonably safe and accessible, the inspection may include:

  • Exterior.
  • Interior.
  • Roof exterior.
  • Roof void.
  • Subfloor.
  • Garage.
  • Carport.
  • Outbuildings included within the inspection agreement.
  • Site improvements.
  • Accessible fencing and retaining walls where relevant.

Inspection Report

Following completion of the inspection, SiteScop will prepare a written report outlining the observations made during the inspection together with photographs, recommendations and comments considered relevant by the inspector.

The report represents the condition of the property only at the date and time of inspection. Property conditions may change after the inspection due to weather, occupancy, maintenance, renovations, timber pest activity or other factors beyond the control of SiteScop.
